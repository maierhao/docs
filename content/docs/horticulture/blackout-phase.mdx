For a few days that I have not planted any Microgreens.
There has been kind of a "Blackout" phase for a few weeks.

Eating without the touch of Microgreens feels like an empty void.
Before going back to growing some more seeds, let's break down one of the coolest concepts during the growth phase. The **Blackout**.

> Concept: **Blackout**
> Growth phase where Microgreens start "reaching" for light.
> When seeds sense no light, they start freaking out and cry for survival.

In total darkness, microgreens seeds do not produce **clorophylle** (the green pigment).
This is totally fine.

But in this state, they are freaking out.
And for this reason, they start the process of **etiolation**.

It is basically a survival instinct saying: *"Hey, either we get some light or we die."*.
So they start growing **taller**, **thinner** and **uniformly** for that quality look at the end, before the light hits.

We are kind of "tricking" the plant making it think we are underground. Controlling its evolution in a precise way. Guiding it to full potential.

### ⏱️ How long to blackout?

Usually **1 to 3 days**, depending on:

* Seed type
* Growth rate
* Desired height and look

Too long = weak, leggy greens.\
Too short = stunted growth.
