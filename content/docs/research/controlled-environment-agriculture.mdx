## Micro-Universe

A Controlled-environment Agriculture (CEA) is like building
an optimised micro-universe where conditions are created
using technology, for life to evolve at its maximum potential.

Inside this micro-universe, we really do not care about outside conditions,
whether they are affected by Climate change, global warming,
water shortages or natural disasters.

<ObsidianCallout type="info">
  <ObsidianCalloutTitle>
    Definition
  </ObsidianCalloutTitle>

  <ObsidianCalloutBody>
    Controlled-environment agriculture (CEA) -- which includes Indoor Agriculture (IA) and Vertical Farming -- consists of crop production systems in greenhouses or other structures that use horticulture and engineering techniques beyond conventional soil-based outdoor production. [#(1)](#1)
  </ObsidianCalloutBody>
</ObsidianCallout>

## The Goal of CEA

The goal of CEA is to build stronger protection from the outdoor environment while providing the optimal conditions of the crops using [Precision Agriculture](./precision-agriculture.mdx) or other innovations.

Achieving long-term food security and environmental sustainability by reducing resources being used while also making the most out of the land.

As many scientists are pointing toward Risks to Life on Land from Global Warning, where changes in temperature, precipitation, and seasonal timing will alter the geographic ranges of many types of plants and animals [#(2)](#2) .

> If warming is kept to 2°C, 18% of insects, 16% of plants, and 8% of vertebrate animals are projected to lose over half of their geographic range [#(2)](#2).

## Energy-Intensive systems

CEA may reduce agricultural water usage by between 53%³ and 98%⁴, and chemical usage by as much as 100%; while using less land to produce the same amount of food as
conventional farming [#(3)](#3).

This is great news, but there is a catch.

One of the main challenges from these systems, is that they are often **energy-intensive** and costly to set up.

This research will also focus on how to make this system as sustainable and resource-efficient as possible.

## Challenges

1. Energy Intensive
2. Cybersecurity Threats to the Digital Ecosystem
3. Initial Cost to Set up
